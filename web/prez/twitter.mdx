# The Blocks: Conclusion ğŸ

Having a **concise set of blocks** allowed the editors to **quickly create** content for their brands. One of the main pain points that we had to consider while creating these blocks, was for editors **to not spend too much time** on these pages.

</Box>

---

<Box sx={{variant: 'layouts.textPage'}}>

#### The Blocks: Conclusion

By only having **4 types** of blocks, the editors quickly were able to design **unique landing pages**. Each block type has **many different layout options**, so the flow of content does not look or feel repetitive.

</Box>

---

<Box sx={{variant: 'layouts.textPage'}}>

#### The Blocks: Conclusion

A **HUGE** win ğŸ¥³ for us turned out to be that each of the 4 block types shared the same atoms (image, headline, deck, byline and date). This allowed for spinning up a new brand with unique styles quickly and efficiently.

</Box>

## Layouts -> Blocks -> Items

</Box>

---

<Box sx={{variant: 'layouts.splitPage'}}>

#### Layouts

- **Layouts** are styles which control the structure of blocks. These are globally set styles that have no theming.

</Box>

---

<Box sx={{variant: 'layouts.splitPage'}}>

#### Layouts -> Blocks

- **Blocks** contain specific styles for the block types. These can be background styles, borders, and how much they break out of our grid.

</Box>

---

<Box sx={{variant: 'layouts.splitPage'}}>

#### Layouts -> Blocks -> Items

- **Items** are styles specific to each individual article or story. These house the styles for Headlines, bylines, decks, and dates

</Box>
